TARGETED BUILD: Galatea, Release 3 / Serial number 040208, by Emily Short,
Copyright (c) 2000-4. Confirmed via our exact game file's banner
(docs/games/galatea.zblorb) — matches the walkthrough sources above, which
all target the same canonical release (this is the final released version;
no divergent editions are documented for this game).

ABOUT THIS GAME'S STRUCTURE:
Galatea is a single-room, single-NPC conversation game (the "Cocktail Party
of the Gods" or "conversational puzzle" subgenre Emily Short pioneered). There
is no map, no inventory puzzles, no traditional parser navigation — the whole
game is ASK/TELL/TOUCH/THINK ABOUT conversation with the statue Galatea, who
has mutable internal state (Mood, Sympathy, Tension, Segue distance — visible
via the debug command VISORX) that shapes her responses and gates ~70 documented
endings. The author has explicitly said she does NOT want players optimizing
toward specific endings — the game is designed to reward organic, curious
conversation. This walkthrough documents ending #64 "Patience" (one of the
warmest, least contentious , and non-randomized endings) as our verified trunk,
while cataloguing the other documented endings/branches for reference.

Below is the full extracted text of the compiled PDF walkthrough (all 70
catalogued endings with whatever command sequences the compiler was able to
verify; entries marked "???" are endings the compiler could not reconstruct
exact commands for and are listed by description only).

1. The protagonist grasps the curtain.
   look behind curtain / get curtain / again
   -> "Not stopping to question..." / heavy tread of approaching feet /
      "You'll never be able to explain this..."

2. Galatea steps off the pedestal and walks away to show the protagonist
   that she is able to walk.
   tell her about exhibit / ask her about pedestal
   -> She steps off and walks away around the corner.

3. Galatea steps off the pedestal and the protagonist escorts her to another
   place so as to talk at length with her.  [commands not reconstructed]

4. Galatea breaks the wrist of the protagonist for turning her a few degrees
   toward himself (for the second time).
   turn her / turn her
   -> She catches your wrist; speaks the reset code EUDOXIA, no response;
      "Broken."

5. Galatea steps off the pedestal to escort the protagonist to someplace so
   as to strip off her gown.
   x placard / a him / z / z / a him / a waking / x her / touch back / x back
   / touch spine / x hair / touch hair / a hair / x dress / touch dress /
   a dress / x hand / touch hand / a hand / kiss hand / galatea, turn / g / g
   / look at her / touch mouth (raises Tension to Considerable) /
   galatea, look at me / z (x100, Sympathy +1 per 5th turn, needs 35+) /
   galatea, take off dress
   -> "It's sewn on. But if you know of someplace quiet where there's a pair
      of scissors..."

6. Galatea steps off the pedestal to run away from the tactless requests of
   the protagonist.  [commands not reconstructed]

7. The protagonist challenges her to infringe the first law of robotics.
   t exhibition / Galatea, kill me
   -> She cannot; philosophical exchange about whether either of them is a
      machine; "You win. For now." — you leave amicably.

8-13. Various "protagonist goes away" endings with different closing
   reflections (remorse over review, adage about details, etc).
   [commands not reconstructed for most; general form is examine/converse a
   little then LEAVE]

14. They both end up talking about their countless experiences. The
    protagonist notes down his phone number to her.
    ("Friendship, with potential: like the former, but not.")
    x her. touch her. galatea, hello. touch back. touch hair. x back.
    touch spine. a her. read placard. a waking. experience. z. x her.
    touch ear. a life. a childhood. a him. a nightmares. a her. z. z.
    galatea, turn around. touch chin. stare at her. g. galatea, turn around.
    x her. x eyes. stare at her. galatea, look at me. t childhood in general.
    t family. t relatives.
    -> She sits on the pedestal, talks about rain/TV; at closing time you
       leave her your phone number on a napkin.

15. They both end up talking about their countless experiences. The
    protagonist does not mind hugging her in front of the caterers.
    ("Friendship: confiding in her.")
    a her. read it. t me. a waking. experience. z. a life. a childhood.
    t childhood. t family. t parents.
    Alternative: t me / t family / again
    -> Ends in a hug goodbye under the caterers' gaze.

16. The protagonist takes her place on the pedestal: he receives deficient
    reviews and Galatea is hired as a helper at the gallery.
    ("Exchange of Places: making yourself the object of inspection.")
    x galatea. touch her. galatea, hello. x placard. a waking. z. a artist.
    g. a sale. a inspection. a travel. a customs. a owners. a exhibit.
    t exhibit. t suicide. hug her. think about owners. enter pedestal.
    -> You trade places with her; she leaves, is later hired by the gallery.

    The protagonist takes her with him.
    ("Hug: ending up on friendly terms, in a different form.")
    NOTE: many routes reach this — hug Galatea any time Mood is not
    Hostile/Sad and Sympathy > 35 (check via VISORX). One verified route:
    x her. a her. read placard. a artist. z. z. a cyprus. a garden. a flowers.
    a smell. a stone. a quarries. a carving. z. a secrets. a carving. x her.
    a punch. a tools. a chisel. g. a polish. a hammer. a marble. a reliefs.
    think about tools. a claw. a drill. a hand. x hand. touch hand. a doubt.
    look at her. a backdrop. g. a pedestal. a spotlight. a air conditioner.
    look. a artist. a sale. a him. a death. t death. z. a screaming.
    a nightmares. a shades. a afterlife. a gods. a prayers. a muses.
    a madness. a drink. a prophecy. a apollo. a delphi. a pythia. a priests.
    a hexameters. a civilization. a anarchy. think about anarchy.
    think about gods. a aphrodite. a love. a pantheon. a hades. z.
    galatea, turn around. a artemis. a zeus. z. a hera. a seduction.
    a dionysus. a drama. a illusions. a freedom. a grief. touch her.
    t grief. t jenny. a him. Recap. think about zeus. a justice.
    a vocabulary. a speech. a breathing. x her. x eyes. x face.
    think about sculpture. a sculpture. a art. a painting. a woman.
    galatea, sorry. hug her.
    -> She steps off the pedestal into your arms; you lead her away.

18. The candor of Galatea comes to the surface; she steps off the pedestal
    and the protagonist takes her elsewhere to talk at length.
    [commands not reconstructed]

19. The protagonist goes away, scared of his own sinister impulse to kill
    her. ("Shame: running up against your own less pleasant qualities.")
    x her. a backdrop. a placard. read placard. a carving. g. a pain.
    a drill. x hands. a hands. a fear. a waking. a death. kill her.
    -> You don't, but the impulse disturbs you; you leave shaken.

20. Galatea admits she was in love with the artist; the protagonist doesn't
    care.
    hello / wait / a love
    -> "So now you know... All the rest of my life's just circumstance."
       You walk away, thinking of the buffet.

21. Galatea admits she was in love with the artist. The protagonist corrects
    her closing statement, giving her back a real smile.
    x her. a her. read placard. a pygmalion. g. a loneliness. a suicide.
    a suicide. think about her. touch her. touch back. touch gown. a gown.
    a owners. a backdrop. x cheek. touch cheek. a carving. g. a pain.
    a polish. a him. a love.
    -> "So far," you reply, and she smiles for real.

22. The protagonist hurts her feelings; she asks him to leave her alone.
    x placard / t it / a him / a him
    -> "Please -- just leave me alone now." You nod and go.

23. Galatea kisses the protagonist (she is crying); he takes her elsewhere
    to talk.
    ("Kiss: just what it sounds like.")
    NOTE: reachable multiple ways — kiss Galatea any time you've decided
    she's not an animate, have a three-quarter view or better, and Tension
    is Considerable (check via VISORX). One verified route:
    x her. a her. touch her. touch her back. a artist. z. z. a studio.
    a ocean. z. x her. x hair. touch hair. a hair. x hand. touch hand.
    a hand. x her. kiss hand. a backdrop. x ear. touch ear. x cheek.
    touch cheek. think about animates. think about her. read placard.
    a waking experience. galatea, turn around. kiss her. g.
    -> She kisses you; "There are things we should discuss, and this is not
       the place."

24-27. Various indifferent/dutiful/burdened farewell variants.
    [commands not reconstructed]

28. Galatea asks if the protagonist is bored; he leaves with a jab about her
    rough edges.
    leave
    -> "Bored so soon?" / "...even your creator would admit you're a bit
       rough around the edges."

29. Galatea asks if he's bored; he leaves, remarking someone else will find
    her. (Sympathy >= 6.)
    read placard. a him. again. a her. x her. touch her. touch back. x back.
    touch spine. x hair. touch hair. leave
    -> "I'm sure someone else will find you before too much longer."

30. Galatea thanks him for the Pygmalion info; he doesn't react.
    read placard / t placard / leave
    -> "Thanks for telling me about Pygmalion" — first time she names him;
       you don't break stride.

31. Same as #30, but with Sympathy >= 6 and having discussed suicide — he
    hesitates half a second before continuing on.
    read placard. a him. again. t suicide. x her. a her. touch her.
    touch back. x back. touch spine. leave
    -> Brief hesitation, then: "This is not your tragedy, you tell yourself
       firmly..."

32. The protagonist leaves while Galatea (now uncooperative) says nothing to
    hold him.
    t exhibit / t komininsky
    -> "Why do you ask me?... my collection of facts to share?" — tense
       silence as you leave.

33. The protagonist invites Galatea to step off the pedestal; she descends.
    ("Invitation: asking her to step out of her milieu.")
    x her. a her. read placard. a suicide. t suicide. galatea, get down. g.
    Alternative: x placard / t placard / galatea, down / again
    -> "Leap of faith," you say. She jumps down; you walk out together.

34. Invited down, she instead becomes nearly obsessed with the artist and
    leaves the protagonist behind.
    [commands not reconstructed]

35. Invited down, she is joyful and intends to confront/reprimand the
    artist.
    a journey. a customs. a sex. galatea, down
    -> She steps down, eager to find and confront Pygmalion, seeming
       younger and more alive.

36. Galatea kills the protagonist.
    ("Pushing Your Luck Too Far: getting yourself killed.")
    x her. touch her. read placard. t suicide. a exhibit. t exhibit.
    a carving. g. g. g. a placard.
    -> You provoke her about the Turing Test / whether the placard is a
       trick; she strangles you.

37. Galatea kills herself.
    ("Delusions: challenging Galatea about her own status.")
    x her. galatea, hello. read placard. a exhibit. t exhibit.
    think about exhibit. think about komininsky. think about galatea.
    galatea, look at galatea.
    Alternative: read placard / t placard / a marble
    -> She demonstrates she can self-terminate and does; only cold marble
       remains.

38. Galatea gets angry with the protagonist because he considers her an
    animate.  [commands not reconstructed]

39. Without warning, Galatea kisses the protagonist.
    ("Exchange of Glances: extremely thorough inspection of the piece.")
    x her. a curtain. x her. x cheek. x ear. touch ear. touch cheek. x her.

41. Galatea becomes sad after the protagonist confesses he is a critic.
    [commands not reconstructed]

42. Galatea prays to Zeus and is made human, then is sick on the
    protagonist.
    ("Mortality: appealing to higher powers.")
    a her. x placard. a him. a afterward. a him. a woman. a church.
    a exorcism. a god. a prayers. a muses. a madness. a prophecy.
    think about the gods. a pantheon. a artemis. a zeus. a hera.
    think about pantheon. a hades. a death. t death. touch her.
    galatea, sorry. a her. a stories. a travels. a numen. x her.
    galatea, turn. a memories. z. a loneliness. z. think. think about her.
    galatea, turn. a death. a thasos. think about gods. galatea, pray to
    zeus.
    -> Zeus answers her prayer; she becomes mortal and immediately vomits
       on you.

43. Galatea prays to Aphrodite; the goddess appears and expresses
    condolences over Pygmalion's death.
    ("Address to Kypris: catching the attention of a deity.")
    x her. x placard. a artist. a carving. z. a him. a fits. a secrets.
    a pirates. a dionysus. a anarchy. a suicide. t suicide. z. a nightmares.
    a moon. a gods. a aphrodite. g.
    -> Aphrodite materializes, denies responsibility for Pygmalion's death,
       comforts Galatea.

44. As #43, but (Mood: Sad, Sympathy: 20, Tension: Considerable, Segue
    distance: 2) Aphrodite hints the protagonist himself is Galatea's hope.
    a aphrodite
    -> Aphrodite: "while you are alive there are always consolations" —
       whispers something to Galatea, whose gaze flickers to you.

45. Aphrodite appears and turns Galatea to stone; the protagonist complains,
    then falls silent.  [commands not reconstructed]

46. As #45, but the protagonist complains more, and Aphrodite mocks him
    ("plenty more fish in the sea").
    Same lead-in as #5, but end on "ask her about Aphrodite" (a aphrodite)
    instead of "galatea, take off dress":
    a aphrodite / a aphrodite
    -> Aphrodite turns Galatea back to lifeless stone and departs, taunting
       the protagonist.

47. As #45/46, but the protagonist is too appalled to speak and huddles
    against the wall.
    a aphrodite / again
    -> Same as #46 but protagonist's reaction is silent horror rather than
       verbal complaint.

48. Galatea discusses sex; the protagonist loses interest in the topic.
    a sex
    -> "No, he didn't [sleep with me]." You get bored of the "sexual angst"
       topic and move on mentally.

49. Galatea prays to Dionysus; the god takes her, offers the same to the
    protagonist, who declines (self-discipline/reluctance).
    ("Bacchanal: catching the attention of a different deity.")
    x placard. a thasos. a gods. a muses. a apollo. a dionysus. a bonds.
    a inhibition. a pain. galatea, pray to dionysus.
    -> Dionysus manifests, frees Galatea, offers the same to the
       protagonist, who (implicitly) doesn't follow.

50. As #49, but the protagonist accepts Dionysus's offer.
    a dionysus. galatea, pray. yes
    -> Protagonist says "yes" to the god's offer and is swept into
       Bacchanalian revelry.

51. As #50 but with hesitation before agreeing (or Dionysus doesn't take
    Galatea at all) — similar dissolution of self either way.
    [commands not reconstructed]

52. The protagonist discovers Galatea is an avatar operated by a human, and
    meets the real "artist."
    ("Wizard of Oz: the 'avatar' ending.")
    x galatea. galatea, hello. x dress. a artist. a waking. z. a him.
    a fits. a nightmares. a shades. a afterlife. a gods. a apollo.
    a dionysus. a illusions. z. a masks.
    -> "You're an avatar, you've got someone controlling you in realtime!"
       — the real operator reveals herself.

53. The protagonist gets angry with Galatea over her inability to grasp his
    views on suicide.  [commands not reconstructed]

54. The protagonist's obsession with the artist tires Galatea; she asks him
    to leave.  [commands not reconstructed]

55. The protagonist gains heightened, uncanny sensitivity to omens from
    Galatea's rambling anecdotes about the artist.
    ("Portents and Omens: access to strange visions.")
    x her. a her. read placard. a artist. z. z. z. a reliefs. a childhood. z.
    (Branches: if she mentions the Tiber -> "a bridge"; the mountains ->
    "a lightning"; the Piraeus -> "a ferry".)
    -> She rambles in increasingly strange, portentous anecdotes; the
       protagonist is left seeing omens everywhere for weeks after.

56-58. Galatea bursts into tears remembering the artist's trip to Crete;
    three variant protagonist reactions (flippant remark and looking away;
    stern rebuke that backfires into her freezing up; stern rebuke followed
    by a comforting touch and hug that succeeds).
    [commands not reconstructed]

59. Asked about her nature, Galatea cycles through material
    transformations (wood, glass, sand, salt, water, flame) before answering
    as a voice: she is what the protagonist thinks she is.
    ("Reflection: confronting her inherent strangeness.")
    x galatea. touch back. touch spine. a her. read placard. a marble.
    a sparkle.
    -> A cascade of "what are you" questions ending in transformations and
       "I am what you think I am; I am what your treatment makes of me."

60. The protagonist condemns the artist's treatment of Galatea until her
    self-esteem rises.  [commands not reconstructed]

61. Galatea confesses she didn't much like the artist but loved his energy,
    and won't lower her expectations of a partner.  [commands not
    reconstructed]

62. The protagonist persuades Galatea to eat (cheese).
    ("Stilton: getting her to eat.")
    galatea, hello. read placard. t suicide. hug her. a her. a waking.
    a sight. a hearing. a sleeping. g. a food. g. g. z. z. t processes.
    -> She samples crackers/Stilton/caviar in the next room, chokes a
       little, then: "This is AMAZING." / "Welcome to humanity."

63. Galatea feels guilty for treating the protagonist unfairly, but still
    asks to be left alone with her thoughts.  [commands not reconstructed]

64. Galatea talks without break; noticing her lack of knowledge, the
    protagonist devotedly decides to instruct her.
    ("Patience: becoming her confidant and teacher.") — OUR VERIFIED TRUNK
    x her. a her. x placard. a artist. a strangeness. a him. t suicide. z.
    a her. a stories. a maxims. a personality. z. z.
    -> Your patient silence acts as a catalyst; she talks and talks. By
       evening's end you're sitting beside her on the pedestal, starting a
       reading list for her education.

65. The protagonist is forced to recall his sister's fatal illness at a bad
    moment; Galatea reaches out but he turns away.
    ("Upset: raising some memories you can't quite handle.")
    x her. a her. read placard. a artist. hug her. attack her.
    think about cancer.
    -> Painful memories of his sister Jenny's death surface; he turns aside
       from Galatea's concern.

66. The protagonist uses Galatea as an ELIZA-style therapist, unburdening
    himself about his losses.
    ("Eliza: using her as a psychiatry program.")
    x galatea. x backdrop. a her. read placard. a artist. a waking. a pain.
    a artist. a loneliness. t suicide. z. a him. a fits. a childhood. z.
    a memories. t me. t family. t parents.
    Alternative: t me / t sister / t family
    -> He pours out losses/frustrations/isolation since "Jenny died"; ends
       feeling wrung out but strangely healed.

67. As #66, but the protagonist ends up disappointing Galatea instead.
    [commands not reconstructed]

68. Using out-of-session knowledge, the player has the protagonist speak the
    reset code "eudoxia," then asks Galatea to list scenarios; she recites
    the first eight before her voice module fails.
    eudoxia / yes
    -> She freezes, then lists: (1) she kills herself, (2) she kills you,
       (3) she departs seeking her artist, (4) she departs seeking other
       exhibits, (5) you end as friends/confidants, (6) you end as lovers,
       (7) you take her place on the pedestal, (8) you offer her a home with
       you — then her voice module stutters out.

69. As #68, but answering "no" to listing scenarios: she resets and erases
    her memory of the encounter.
    eudoxia / no
    -> She begins resetting herself for the next visitor, forgetting
       everything.

70. The player has the protagonist speak a magic word (xyzzy/zork/plugh),
    breaking the fourth wall entirely.
    xyzzy
    -> Reveals the protagonist is themselves an avatar operated remotely by
       an unseen tester/player, mid-experiment, being asked whether the
       Galatea "product" will sell. ("Too cerebral.")

DEBUG / META COMMANDS mentioned by the compiled walkthrough (author-provided,
useful for probing the conversation state during hint authoring — NOT part
of ordinary play and should not be taught to players as "the" solution):
- TOPICLISTX — lists all topics in the game and the verbs that work with
  them.
- VISORX — switches the status bar to display live stats on the conversation
  (Mood, Sympathy, Tension, Segue distance, etc.) which many endings are
  gated on.
