Sources used
------------
1. CASA (solutionarchive.com) walkthrough, file id 11126
   https://www.solutionarchive.com/file/id,11126/
   Author/contributor: Richard Bos, August 2014.
   Content (as fetched/summarized — CASA declines full verbatim reproduction via automated
   fetch, but the core command sequence was extracted):
     "answer phone, stand. s, remove watch, remove clothes, drop all, enter shower.
      take watch, wear watch, n, get all from table. open dresser, get clothes,
      wear clothes. e, s, enter car, drive."
   At the driving decision point: "no, wait, no, wait, yes" (one branch — leaves Las Mesas
   without incident) vs. answering "yes" at the Loungent Technologies stop prompt (the
   "major clue"/twist-revealing branch): "open wallet, take card, insert card in slot,
   enter cubicle, read note, take form and pen, sign form, out, west".
   CASA also flags an early clue: "at this point you can look under the bed and examine
   what you find there" (right after getting up, in the Bedroom).
   CASA's own framing: the walkthrough explicitly warns that following it "misses the
   point of 9:05" — the losing/twist branches are the actual content of the game, and a
   naive "get to the ending" playthrough undersells it.

Build-match assessment
-----------------------
No separate "our build" walkthrough exists — 9:05 has only ever had two released Inform
versions (v1.01 initial, then a bugfix bump to v1.11/v1.12 sometime after 2000, both by
Adam Cadre, no gameplay/puzzle changes between them per IFDB/IFWiki — only text/bug fixes).
The CASA walkthrough (undated build target, 2014) and our v1.11 build both replayed
identically for every command tried; no build-drift was observed. Confidence: HIGH — this
is effectively the only walkthrough content that exists for this very short game, and it
verified command-for-command (with a few noun/verb disambiguations added — see
905.cmds.txt) against our exact file.

Derived, verified command list: see 905.cmds.txt (verified against tools/play.cjs --strict,
exit 0).

Game structure note (for the caller): 9:05 is designed around alternate/failure paths as
its core content — the CASA walkthrough itself says the straight-line "success" ending
(escape via freeway) is the least interesting outcome, and the game rewards deliberately
choosing "wrong" answers and examining things (the corpse, the wallet, the note) to reveal
the twist: you are not the homeowner "Hadley" waking up late for work — you are the
burglar who killed Hadley overnight, and you sleepily set off to imitate the victim's
morning routine and go to his job. See 905.puzzle-notes.md for the full analysis.
