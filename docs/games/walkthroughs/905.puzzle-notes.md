# 9:05 — puzzle-logic notes

Slugs match the `## [slug]` markers in `905.cmds.txt`. This game has no traditional
"puzzles" to solve — its entire content is a single narrative twist and a small set of
branch points that either reveal it (worth taking) or paper over it (the "clean" ending).
These notes exist to make sure a hint author teaches the *twist-discovery method*, not the
commands, and never spoils the reveal itself in an early-game hint.

## ⚠ RANDOMIZED BETWEEN PLAYS

**Nothing is randomized.** 9:05 has no `@random` gates, no per-run item placement, and no
seed-dependent values. The trunk in `905.cmds.txt` replays bit-identical on every run,
seeded or not. This is a fixed narrative-branch game, not a puzzle-with-solution game — the
"correct answer" per branch is a fixed yes/no choice, not a derived value.

## Core mechanic: the game IS the twist, not a puzzle to solve

9:05 opens as an utterly mundane "you overslept, hurry to work" scenario. There is no
conventional obstacle to overcome — every command in the main line succeeds immediately
(shower, dress, drive). The entire design intent (confirmed by the CASA walkthrough's own
disclaimer, which explicitly warns players away from following it) is that the *real*
content is discovered by:
1. Examining things the "hurry up, you're late" framing discourages you from examining
   (the corpse under the bed, the wallet's contents, the office note), and
2. Deliberately choosing options other than the "obvious, get-to-work-fastest" one at the
   two yes/no decision points while driving.

A player who just follows the surface instructions ("get up, shower, dress, drive to
work") reaches a bland, low-content escape ending and never learns anything. **The hint
ladder should nudge investigation and unhurried choices before it nudges any specific
command.**

## [wake-and-clue] Wake up, discover the body

**The hintable nugget:** the game's opening narration (phone call demanding you rush to
work) is deliberately designed to make players *skip* investigating their surroundings.
The single most important discovery in the entire game — `look under bed` in the Bedroom,
immediately after standing up — reveals "the corpse of the guy who owns this house." This
is the load-bearing clue for the whole twist; a walkthrough/hint that omits it teaches
nothing.

- A hint ladder here should progress: (1) "the framing wants you to hurry — try slowing
  down and looking around instead," → (2) "is there anywhere in this room you haven't
  looked, like under something?" → (3) explicit `look under bed`. Never state the
  conclusion ("it's a body") in an early rung — let the command's own output deliver the
  reveal.
- `x me` right after is optional color (confirms you're "covered with mud and dried
  sweat," reinforcing that you did something violent last night) but not load-bearing;
  it's cheap and worth keeping for the art/prose pipeline, though not essential to hint.

## [shower-and-dress] Bathroom routine

No twist content here — this is pure "getting ready" business (remove old clothes/watch,
shower, dress in clean clothes, put watch back on). Fully linear, no choices. Nothing to
hint. Sequence matters only in that you must be undressed and empty-handed to "enter shower"
(probed 2026-07-01: watch on → "You don't think the watch you're wearing is waterproof.";
carrying items → "Carrying your possessions into the shower is a bad idea." — so DROP ALL is
genuinely load-bearing). The watch does NOT have to go back on: a full run skipping
`take watch`/`wear watch` entirely still reaches the twist ending (the earlier claim here
that skipping it breaks the wallet/keys pickup was probe-falsified).

## [leave-house] Exit the house

Also linear/no-choice: open front door (note: "door" alone is ambiguous between "front
door" and "bedroom door" in this build — must disambiguate by naming "front door"),
`unlock car` (the parser infers "with the keys" you're carrying — no need to spell out
"with keys"), `enter car`.

## [drive-decision] Freeway-or-Loungent decision point

**The hintable nugget:** while driving, the game asks two sequential yes/no questions —
first about a freeway onramp, then (if you say no to the freeway) about stopping at
"Loungent Technologies," which the intro phone call already named as your workplace.
Saying **yes** to Loungent leads to the twist-revealing ending; saying **yes** to the
freeway (or "wait," which the parser rejects and just re-prompts) leads to a short,
low-content "you have left Las Mesas" ending that dead-ends the story early.

- ✗ Some published walkthrough phrasing (CASA) uses a padded sequence "no, wait, no, wait,
  yes" against an older release (v1.01/serial 9502) — the "wait" answers are simply
  rejected by the "please answer yes or no" reprompt and consume no game state; they are
  walkthrough padding, not required input. ✓ On our v1.11 build the minimal, equivalent
  input is just **no** (skip the freeway), then **yes** (stop at Loungent).
- Hint framing: "the phone call already told you where you work — did the game offer you
  a way to actually go there?" rather than naming the exact yes/no sequence.

## [loungent-twist] Sign the form — twist confirmed and game ends

**The hintable nugget:** at Loungent, using the wallet's **ID card** (not the driver's
license — the wallet holds both) unlocks the employee entrance. Inside, a cubicle marked
"HADLEY" (the name the opening phone call addressed you by) has a note from "MB" (Matthew
Bowman, whose name is on the office door to the west) demanding a signed form. Taking and
signing the form, then walking west into Bowman's office to deliver it, triggers the full
reveal: Bowman doesn't recognize you, security is called, and a news broadcast explicitly
narrates the twist — you are the burglar who killed the homeowner ("Hadley") overnight,
stashed the body under the bed, and then groggily tried to live out the victim's morning
routine including going to his job.

- This is the "full" ending — it's the one worth treating as the trunk completion for
  hints/room-facts, since it's the only branch that shows the Loungent Technologies
  interior locations (parking lot, hallway, cubicle) and delivers the payoff text.
- ✗ CASA's phrasing uses "take card" — our build accepts `take id` (the object is named
  "ID card" in room/inventory text); either phrasing would likely resolve via the parser's
  synonym matching, but `take id` is what was verified.
- **Branch not encoded in the trunk:** answering "yes" to the *first* freeway prompt (or
  "yes"/"no" combinations that lead to leaving Las Mesas) produces the short "You have left
  Las Mesas" ending with no twist reveal and no jail/news text — a valid but much less
  interesting completion. Not worth a separate cmds file; noted here for completeness only.
- **Not explored/left as an open thread:** the ClubFloyd v1.01 transcript's second,
  longer playthrough examines additional objects (driver's license specifically, looking
  in the wallet before opening it, examining the trunk) that weren't re-verified against
  our v1.11 build since they're flavor-only and don't gate any branch. If room-facts
  generation wants deeper Loungent-parking-lot/office description text later, those extra
  examine verbs are a reasonable place to probe first.
