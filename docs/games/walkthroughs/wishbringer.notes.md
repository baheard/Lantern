# Wishbringer — walkthrough analysis notes

Build: release 69 / serial 850920 (`docs/games/wishbringer.z3`). Companion to
`wishbringer.txt` (raw sources) and `wishbringer.cmds.txt` (verified command list).
These are the puzzle-logic calls that the published walkthroughs got wrong or left implicit
on our build. Hints must teach the *method* here, never the commands or random values.

**For the hint author (`generate-hints`):** each `##` section below is a puzzle. The bolded
*insight* is the hintable nugget — graduate it (nudge → method → near-answer), and **never**
emit the literal command, the `@random` value, or the build-specific name. Build-divergence
notes (✗ published / ✓ our build) flag where a copied walkthrough would mislead a player.

## ⚠ RANDOMIZED BETWEEN PLAYS (hint-critical — read first)
Anything here differs every real play, so **a hint must teach the method, never state the value**
(the value a real player sees won't match the command list).

- **The pelican's power word** — the magic word the lighthouse traces on the cloud after
  `give the hat to the bird`, later used at the Tower as `say <word>`. It is `@random`-generated
  per playthrough (observed: FRATTO, KALUZE, SORKIN). In `wishbringer.cmds.txt` it is hard-coded
  as `say fratto` **only because the harness seeds its RNG** (`--seed 1` → always "fratto"); that
  is a test-determinism artifact, NOT a value to ever surface to a player. Hint phrasing must be
  *"say the word the lighthouse showed you."*

Nothing else in this solution is known to be randomized — the puzzles below are deterministic
(boot-patrol timing is clock-driven but consistent within a run, not random). If a future probe
with `--random` over several runs surfaces another varying element, add it here.

## Misty Island exit (the published-walkthrough bug)
After the platypus King gives you the wizard's hat, **stay in the Throne Room** and
`wait, blow whistle`. The King's fanfare is what transports you back — the whistle "only
twinkles at Edge of Lake" (Invisiclues), so it does nothing if you've stepped out onto the
beach first. Several sources print `get hat, e, blow whistle`; that `e` strands you on Misty
Island where every exit is blocked (N/S = cliff, E = lake/drown, W = back to the King → defy
him → "granola mines" death). Correct sequence: `get the hat`, `wait`, `blow the whistle`.

## Boot Patrol timing (Witchville, after dark)
Once Witchville falls under the Tower, the Boot Patrol roams and arrests you (→ Jail Cell) if
it catches you on a street after curfew. It converges on **Rotary East within ~1 turn** of you
arriving there from the Park. Two consequences for the command list:
- Do the **fountain-token + cinema** errand early (~6:40 PM, right after the underground stump
  loop), not after the pelican — by ~6:56 PM the boots are already on top of Rotary East.
- A single `wait` at the Park before stepping `e` shifts the patrol cycle just enough to slip
  into the theater. Buy the ticket *outside* (`buy ticket` at Rotary East, auto-uses the gold
  coin) the same turn, then `in` immediately — Miss Voss phones the boots the moment she sees
  the coin.

## Token comes from the fountain, not the arcade
The brass token is in the **Park fountain**, guarded by a piranha: `put worm in fountain`
(piranha grabs the worm) then `get token`. The arcade ("All Games One Token") only consumes it.

## 3D glasses double-life
Get the glasses from under a seat **inside** the theater (`examine floor`, `get glasses`) — the
free-carton in the lobby is a red herring (`get glasses` there fails). Wearing them resolves the
movie *and*, later, the Tower's upper "Fuzziness" room — but **never walk while wearing them**
(every direction = "too blurry"). `remove glasses` before moving.

## Arcade joystick — z3 parser note
`again` (the AGAIN/G verb) is unreliable in this build; spell out each push:
`push joystick west`, `push joystick west`, `push joystick south`, `push joystick south`,
`press button`, then `yes`, `yes`. Winning the game flies you to Hilltop at the Tower (+5).

## Power word is per-run @random (determinism cutoff)
The pelican's lighthouse word (`give the hat to the bird` → "traces a word … : XXXXXX") is
generated by `@random`, so it differs every run (FRATTO, KALUZE, SORKIN seen in testing). The
linear command list **cannot** hard-code `say <word>` past this point and stay valid. Under the
harness's seeded RNG (`--seed`, default 1) it *is* reproducible, so a seeded trunk can include
`say <word>` — but **hint content must teach "say the word the lighthouse showed you," never a
value**, because a real player in the app gets a true-random word.

## Tower entry — the violet note drives Mr. Crisp off (it's not a bribe)
`say <word>` at Hilltop lowers the drawbridge; `s` drops you into the **Torture Chamber**,
chained, with Mr. Crisp **looting your inventory one item per turn** (the small glowing stone
he'd pocket *is* the Wishbringer — losing it makes the game unwinnable). **Insight:** the violet
note is a love-letter from "Violet" (Miss Voss) to Crisp; once it reaches him he reads it, melts
("*Violet scolds me when I'm late!*"), drops the lab coat, and **scampers off up the ladder** —
removing the threat. Two ways it plays:
- `give violet note to mr crisp` proactively → he leaves *immediately*, before looting the stone (safest).
- Just hold it → he loots a few items first (horseshoe etc., which fall to the floor and are
  re-gettable) and only flees when he finally grabs the note himself — riskier for the stone's looting order.
Either way the note ends up gone (he takes it), so anything you need *from* the note must be
learned by **reading it first**.

## "Corky" is Crisp's pet-name, not the dog's (published-walkthrough trap)
**Build divergence / trap:** ✗ published walkthroughs say to command the cottage hellhound with
**"Alexis, heel"** — wrong for release 69. Reading the violet note shows the name **"Corky"**,
but **that's the embarrassing nickname Violet calls *Mr. Crisp*** — saying `corky` only gets
"*Mr. Crisp hates it when you use that name!*"; it does **not** pacify the dog. So the note is a
red herring *for the dog puzzle* (its real job is driving Crisp off, above).

## Free the Princess, climb out
`get lab coat` → `look in pocket` reveals a rusty key → `get rusty key` →
`unlock chain with rusty key` (you're chained; can't move until freed) → `pull down lever`
frees Princess Tasmania (she vanishes in light, +score). Then `open hatch`, `u` → **Round
Chamber**, `u` again → the top room.

## The Laboratory hides behind "Fuzziness" — glasses gate movement
**Insight:** the top room reads as **"Fuzziness"** (can't see, can't move any direction) until
you `wear glasses` — then it resolves into the **Laboratory** (telescope, control panel, sleeping
black cat, a useless broom). You must keep the glasses **on** to navigate the lab and the stairs
down to the Round Chamber; `remove glasses` up there = blind again, and any held cat squirms free.
Remove them only once you're back in the (clear) Round Chamber. The **black cat in the lab is a
red herring** — it isn't Chaos and can't be carried out; the real cat is recreated later.

## Control panel: the SECOND switch matters, the broom doesn't
Two labeled switches. **Insight:** the **second** switch cuts power to the library's glass case
(needed before you can break it) — `push second switch` (+score). The **first** switch is just
"Palace Theater" (the cinema), irrelevant to winning. The **broom is a deliberate red herring**
(only tied to the optional, sub-optimal Flight wish).

## Exit the tower — the drawbridge crank is hidden behind a painting
The Vestibule drawbridge is closed and `open drawbridge` says *"the mechanism is hidden."*
**Insight:** the Round Chamber's 13 blackened paintings conceal it — `look behind paintings`
reveals a **metal crank**; `turn crank` opens the drawbridge (+score). Then `n` (Vestibule), `n`
(across — it slams shut behind you) → Hilltop. (Published walkthroughs skip this entirely with a
bare `n,n,e`, assuming the bridge is already open — a real player will be stuck here.)

## Endgame (village) — VERIFIED end-to-end (95/100)
From Hilltop `e` → Outside Cottage. Command the hellhound with `alexis, heel` (name from the
re-read opened note, above) → enter cottage → `get steel key` → library →
`unlock library door with steel key` → `break case with horseshoe` (case must be unpowered —
see second switch) → `get sculpture` → `put wishbringer in sculpture` → `yes`/`yes` (the
objecting "Old Woman" is the Evil One's sister/the real Old Woman — proceed; this recreates
Chaos) → `get cat` → `knock on magick shoppe door`. **WIN: 95/100, 1 wish used.** Full list
verified `--strict --seed 1` clean from scratch.

## OPEN PUZZLE — the last 5 points (free the platypus WITHOUT the Rain wish) → 100/100
The canonical list uses `wish for rain` to flood the pit at Lake Edge so the trapped platypus
floats out and draws the X (whistle). That costs 1 of 7 Wishes — and **5 points + the "zero
wishes" ending**. The documented 100/100 path frees the platypus with a **branch** instead.
**Method (confirmed mechanics, full path NOT yet verified):**
- At **Cliff Bottom** (the gnarled tree), `break branch` works → a ~6-ft dead branch. It's the
  reach tool for the 5-ft-deep pit; use it at Lake Edge in place of `open umbrella / wish for rain`.
- **Catch #1 — load limit:** `get branch` fails ("Your load is too heavy") while holding
  can+horseshoe+coin+note+umbrella. Must shed an item to carry the branch.
- **Catch #2 — the umbrella isn't disposable:** opening the umbrella at the pit is what springs
  the **cemetery iron gate** (needed to go south later) — separate from the rain wish. So you
  can't simply drop it; the no-wish path has to sequence branch-carry vs. gate-open vs. load.
- **Unverified:** the exact verb to free the platypus with the branch (`free platypus with branch`
  / `put branch in pit` / `lift platypus with branch` — untested), and a load-feasible ordering.
